Overview

What is Signify?

Signify is a mobile application designed to translate sign language into text and speech in real-time. Using advanced gesture recognition technology, the app bridges the communication gap between individuals who are hearing-impaired and non-signers, enabling smoother interactions. The primary goal of Signify is to enhance accessibility for those with speech or hearing impairments, making communication more inclusive and accessible to everyone.

What Mobile App
When Aug 2024 – Oct 2024
Why We Created This App

The idea for Signify emerged from the growing need to support communication between the hearing-impaired and those unfamiliar with sign language. We created this app to provide a solution that fosters seamless communication and inclusivity, reducing communication difficulties and improving the quality of life for the broader community.

DISCOVER, CREATE, INSPIRE

User
Research

User research for the Signify app focused on the needs of deaf individuals and those learning sign language. Findings showed users want quick, accurate translations and learning tools. Challenges include varying sign language comprehension and tech unfamiliarity. Suggested improvements include a teaching module and a more accessible user interface to enhance the app's effectiveness.

03
COMMUNICATION FOR ALL

Ideation

The inspiration behind Signify comes from a personal connection with individuals who are hearing and speech-impaired. Communicating with them, I realized the profound need to bridge the gap between sign language users and those unfamiliar with it. The goal of Signify is to make communication seamless, empowering these individuals to connect with society on equal terms and enhancing the quality of life for everyone.

Signify is designed to translate sign language into text and speech in real-time, making daily interactions easier and fostering inclusion. By breaking down communication barriers, the app serves as a tool to create a more understanding and accessible world, ensuring that everyone, regardless of ability, can fully engage in the community around them.

Key Insight
Communication barriers significantly impact the daily lives of hearing-impaired individuals, often leading to social isolation.
